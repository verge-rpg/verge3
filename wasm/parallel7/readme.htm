Readme 2: This Time It's Personal

Good *God*, is it that time already? It's something-o'clock. Can't tell. Too tired. Whaever it is, it has an "am" in it so that must be bad. Living in England, the HOV competition ended at 5am and I made the most of the time. My fingers now WOBBLE when I try to touch things.

Zip (the guy you can thank for the battle system) and I have been working tirelessly to throw our little contribution together in some playable form so you can actually get a go on what we've been whittling away at for the past fortnight.

Welcome to Parallel 7. It's a Sci-fi horror RPG but the horror doesn't really hit until towards the end of this sadly unfinished entry (I do plan to do some work on it post-HOV, so you may well see it finished in the not-so-distant future). The battle system works, the menu systems function as they should but the plot - as you may notice while playing - will stop abruptly. This is where *I* stopped abruptly and submitted. Time, eh? There's never enough of it. Or productivity, for that matter.

The game litterally sticks you in the boots of Hugo Zane, failed bounty hunter extraordinaire, as he's dragged around a passenger liner by a woman with nice hair. You also get to hit people and things, which is a bonus in my book, which happens to be a battered copy of Fight Club, in which hitting people features prevelantly.

Sorry, where was I? Yes: game. You play. You like. Game make happy happy. Tarzan like Jane. Or something.

Comments and questions are heavily encouraged (interference22@hotmail.com or v-rpg.com's hallowed message boards) since a whole lot of time was spent on this and - while not half what I wanted it to finally look like - is still something I'm fairly proud of as a first HOV attempt. The dialogue, art and code is all original. Feel free to steal the code. Troupe made some of the music (the .OXMs in the music directory, like the title theme) so that is also fairly nice to experience.
